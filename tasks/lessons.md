# Lessons

- 2026-04-18: A real LFM2 `full_attention` layer compiling through `HybridDecodeKernelSet` on ANE does NOT imply that a mixed-runtime serving lane is semantically usable.
  Rule: after any single-layer hybrid or ANE probe succeeds, run a real free-form text rollout before keeping the architecture splice. On `LiquidAI/LFM2.5-350M`, the attention-layer probe compiled and executed cleanly, but the six-layer mixed-runtime decode collapsed into `a little girl named ...` loops and had to be killed despite the apparent runtime viability.

- 2026-04-18: On the retained exact LFM2 lane, long-horizon throughput was more sensitive to the attention-context implementation than the short `128`-token benchmark suggested.
  Rule: when a profile shows a scalar `Q*K^T` / `V*softmax` cache loop on the hot path, optimize that visible-token-dependent loop first and re-measure on a long coherent rollout, not only the short benchmark. Rewriting `decodeContextFromCaches` to BLAS-backed matrix-vector ops moved the retained long-horizon `Once upon a time` run from `46.78 tok/s` to repeated `56.74-56.86 tok/s` while preserving coherent output.

- 2026-04-18: Small single-run wins in classifier partition size are too noisy to justify a new default on this repo.
  Rule: treat `ESPRESSO_CLASSIFIER_ARGMAX_BLOCK_SIZE` or similar tuning knobs as research surfaces until at least two repeated same-contract runs show a stable edge. On LFM2, `8192` briefly beat the default `4000` block size once but failed to repeat strongly enough to change the default.

- 2026-04-18: A BLAS-backed speedup in one LFM2 hot loop does not generalize automatically to the rest of the exact CPU lane.
  Rule: do not replace generic `vDSP_mmul` matvec helpers with direct `cblas_sgemv` just because the attention-cache rewrite improved. On the retained `LiquidAI/LFM2.5-350M` lane, swapping the shared `multiplyRowMajorMatrix` helper to `cblas_sgemv` dropped the long coherent benchmark from `56.74-56.86 tok/s` to `44.70 tok/s`, so every BLAS substitution must be justified by the exact shape and call pattern it serves.

- 2026-04-18: Throughput benchmarks on this repo cannot be parallelized without contaminating the result.
  Rule: never run two long tok/s jobs concurrently on the same host. A mistakenly parallelized LFM2 control/candidate pair collapsed from the mid-50 tok/s range to roughly `36.7-36.9 tok/s`, which made the first comparison unusable.

- 2026-04-18: Reordering partitioned-classifier blocks by descending max row norm is too weak to matter on the retained LFM2 lane.
  Rule: do not keep classifier scheduling heuristics unless they clear a material repeated win on the long coherent contract. On `LiquidAI/LFM2.5-350M`, `ESPRESSO_CLASSIFIER_ORDER_BLOCKS_BY_MAX_NORM=1` preserved exactness and coherence but moved the retained long run only from `55.29 tok/s` to `55.39 tok/s`, which is noise, not a publishable gain.

- 2026-04-18: Hugging Face BPE tokenizers do not always encode BOS as `<|begin_of_text|>` or inline numeric `ids` on `TemplateProcessing` entries.
  Rule: when importing a new tokenizer-json model family, resolve BOS/prefix tokens through the `post_processor.special_tokens` map as well as direct `ids`, and accept alternate BOS token names such as `<|startoftext|>`. Otherwise Espresso can silently drop BOS, appear parity-broken, and report meaningless throughput on the wrong prompt contract.

- 2026-04-18: When the user constrains a benchmark or optimization campaign to a specific model family, keep every baseline, experiment, and claim on that exact model until the user changes scope again.
  Rule: do not silently switch to the repo's strongest historical lane just because it has a higher throughput ceiling. Treat off-model runs as out-of-scope research noise, update `tasks/todo.md` immediately, and restart the retained baseline on the requested model before making more changes.

- 2026-04-18: On the current `LiquidAI/LFM2.5-350M` exact-CPU lane, the raw FP16 tiled classifier is slower than the partitioned FP32 classifier despite looking like the more specialized path.
  Rule: do not assume the llama large-vocab head policy transfers to LFM2. Force both backends on the real artifact before choosing a default, and keep the benchmark label tied to the backend actually executed rather than the nominal strategy.

- 2026-04-18: A long-horizon publication-style prompt suite and a recurrent ANE microbench are not interchangeable just because both report tok/s.
  Rule: if the question is output coherence, only use text-serving prompt/horizon contracts. Treat recurrent-scaling or synthetic ANE microbenches as runtime-economics evidence only; they cannot validate or invalidate narrative quality for a dense model like LFM2.

- 2026-04-18: When the user asks for the maximum number on a specific model, optimize that model directly instead of redirecting to a different family with a better known ceiling.
  Rule: keep the throughput campaign anchored to the named model and define separate lanes for `max raw number`, `max coherent number`, and `publication-shape number` if needed, rather than substituting a different architecture.

- 2026-04-18: Tiny ANE probe shapes can lie about the real LFM2 conv path.
  Rule: do not treat `dim=32` / `lane=8` grouped-conv behavior as evidence for `LiquidAI/LFM2.5-350M`. Re-run ANE parity probes at the actual LFM2 width (`dim=1024`) with bounded FP16 amplitudes before keeping or killing the short-conv lane.

- 2026-04-18: The LFM2 short-conv ANE kernel can be parity-correct if the recurrent state contract carries a full previous chunk instead of a tiny `spatial=2` output.
  Rule: for the current ANE lane, keep `convStateIn` / `convStateOut` at full `laneSpatial`, slice the last `kernelWidth - 1` positions inside the kernel, and avoid tiny ANE output surfaces for recurrent carry state.

- 2026-04-18: A previously-killed ANE kernel path can become viable again once the runtime contract is corrected.
  Rule: after fixing the state contract or probe shape, re-run semantic parity on the original implementation before locking in a slower fallback. On LFM2 short-conv, the direct generic `k=3` kernel became both parity-correct and faster than the factorized fallback under the full-chunk state contract.

- 2026-04-12: Do not build new Espresso import paths on top of `EdgeRunner` when the dependency is already slated for removal.
  Rule: if a model-conversion feature needs parser, tensor-mapping, or BLOBFILE-writing logic that currently exists in `EdgeRunner`, cherry-pick only the minimal logic into Espresso-owned code and keep the new path dependency-free from the start.

- 2026-04-10: If a repo needs to serve both Claude-style tooling and agents that read `AGENTS.md`, do not maintain two separate copies of the project contract.
  Rule: keep the shared execution policy in root `AGENTS.md`, make root `CLAUDE.md` import `@AGENTS.md`, and reserve `CLAUDE.md` for Claude-specific notes only. This avoids drift and keeps benchmark-policy changes in one place.
- 2026-04-10: Cross-repo tok/s comparisons are misleading when model size, hardware class, and deployment target differ materially.
  Rule: never compare Espresso's `stories110m` M3 Max numbers directly against a phone-class `2B` model result and treat the larger headline as "better." First normalize by model scale, device class, and product target; otherwise the comparison overstates Espresso's relative strength and understates the public-deployment value of the phone result.
- 2026-04-10: A shared Claude setup is not real if `.gitignore` still hides it.
  Rule: if the repo adopts checked-in `CLAUDE.md`, `AGENTS.md`, `.claude/rules/`, `.claude/commands/`, or `.claude/settings.json`, keep only machine-local files ignored: `CLAUDE.local.md`, `.claude/settings.local.json`, archived Claude worktrees, and obvious filesystem noise.
- 2026-04-10: Do not let recurrent-hybrid metadata support imply training or checkpoint support. A proof-config parser that can load `grouped-dual-state-v1` is useful, but if the RWKV student/checkpoint code can still instantiate or round-trip that spec, the repo starts lying about what exists.
  Rule: keep proof-contract validation and actual runtime/checkpoint support as separate boundaries, and add refusal tests the moment a new cell family enters config space.
- 2026-04-10: Richer recurrent state is not monotonic. In the grouped dual-state ANE prototype, `12x64` and `12x128` memory widths beat the RWKV control materially, but `12x192` fell back below RWKV.
  Rule: sweep `stateChannelsPerHead` on the same host and harness before treating a richer-state cell as viable. Past the state-width knee, extra memory capacity can erase the runtime win quickly.
- 2026-04-10: ANE recurrent microbench absolute tok/s can drift materially across immediate reruns even with `ANE_COMPILE_CACHE_POLICY=preferCached`.
  Rule: do not keep or kill a runtime prototype on one lucky absolute number. Use paired repeated control/prototype runs and judge the relative edge, not a single tok/s headline.
- 2026-04-10: A single-surface "associative-state" prototype that keeps the same 2-input/2-output recurrent session shape as RWKV is too close to the existing vector-state economics to justify the replacement path.
  Rule: if a new recurrent micro-runtime is within low-single-digit percent of the current RWKV baseline on the same host and harness, do not keep polishing it. Escalate to a richer state contract, likely multi-surface or explicitly per-head associative state, before spending training effort.
- 2026-04-10: When a new recurrent cell family first passes unit and hardware tests, prove it again through the actual release CLI and a retained artifact before treating the lane as real.
  Rule: tests establish local correctness, but the first durable keep decision for a new proof runtime must come from an end-to-end artifact run through the shipping binary surface, with the artifact and summary retained in-repo.
- 2026-04-10: Recurrent export metadata cannot assume the checkpoint lives beside `metadata.json`.
  Rule: when `recurrentWeightsRef` points into a nested path like `weights/recurrent.bin`, the runtime metadata lookup must search ancestor export directories and match on the referenced checkpoint filename, or the probe can silently fall back to the wrong loader and report fake format failures.
- When the user says to use subagents throughout the session, switch immediately to a subagent-assisted workflow and keep parallel agent coverage active for the rest of the session instead of treating delegation as optional.
- When the user says to finish the entire implementation, do not reinterpret that as “finish the next milestone.” Keep going until the full requested scope is actually implemented and fully re-verified, or explicitly flag a hard blocker.
- When wrapping shell commands in `zsh`, never use the special read-only variable name `status` for exit capture. Use a neutral name like `exit_code`.
- When adding an optional acceleration path, guard both build-time and runtime integration separately: missing toolchains must not break `swift build`, and missing runtime prerequisites must fall back to the stable path instead of throwing.
- When loading optional native code, never search from the current working directory. Restrict discovery to explicit overrides or trusted build/executable-relative paths so local path injection cannot influence `dlopen`.
- Do not invoke `swift package` recursively from SwiftPM test processes against the same package. It can deadlock on the package/build lock; prefer text-based manifest checks or an external integration script.
- When benchmarking an optional native acceleration path, force the comparison through separate processes and expose exact branch-point counters. Otherwise you risk comparing two fallback runs or masking the real source of the speedup.
- Do not promote a benchmark-affecting behavior change from an ad hoc local suite run. For Espresso, a throughput win only counts if it clears the retained publishable harness and baseline-comparison gates on the actual release command.
- Do not treat a `128`-token publication suite as runnable on the retained Stories Core ML `seq128` baseline. In the current compare harness, any non-empty prompt plus `128` generated tokens exceeds that contract, so this is a benchmark-lane blocker, not a model-performance result.

Even with a compatible `seq256` Core ML export, the current exact hybrid llama lane can exhaust the per-process ANE compile budget mid-suite, so prompt-level process isolation or exec-restart/resume has to be part of the benchmark design.
- Historical recurrent artifact families can carry stale sidecar formats even when the recurrent checkpoint still loads. Validate `future-sidecar.bin` compatibility with the current `TwoStepStudentCheckpoint` loader before treating an old artifact family as rerunnable evidence.
- When a benchmark harness regression appears immediately after a source change, confirm the release binary is actually rebuilt before debugging the logic. In this session the subprocess parser was already fixed in source and test-covered; the failing behavior came from a stale `.build/release/espresso-generate`.
- If a recurrent training/export path seems missing entirely, check git history before rebuilding it from scratch. Espresso had a deleted `scripts/distill_stories_rwkv.py` that was recoverable and materially shortened the Phase 3 unblock.
- Do not assume exact-lane token drift on Stories is an ANE-classifier-only problem. On the retained `fox_return` failure, forcing `cpu_fp16_tiled`, `cpu_partitioned_fp32`, or even `ESPRESSO_USE_CPU_EXACT_DECODE=1` did not restore parity with the seq256 Core ML baseline, so the investigation has to include trunk/prefill/numerics paths.
- Do not trust the custom `tokenizer.model` contract for new recurrent research just because it exists in the bundle. On the Stories bundle, the recovered recurrent path was training and probing against a tokenizer that encoded the sample prompt as `49` tokens, while the retained `tokenizer.json` contract encoded the same prompt as `12`. Recurrent quality conclusions were invalid until the teacher path was switched to prefer `tokenizer.json` and the probe could accept exact prompt token IDs.
- A `3-layer` recurrent cell hitting `800+ tok/s` is not close to success if it still collapses into long token runs after the tokenizer contract is fixed. For the publishable `128`-token goal, treat repeated-run collapse after a short coherent prefix as an architecture kill signal, not an invitation for endless local tuning.
- 2026-04-10: Do not route real HF `tokenizer.model` assets through Espresso's in-tree `SentencePieceTokenizer` just because the filename matches. That implementation reads Espresso's compact fixture/export format, not the full HF SentencePiece contract, and it can silently produce byte-fragmented prompt IDs.
  Rule: for real artifact prompt-text work, prefer the authoritative `tokenizer.json` path when it exists, and use exact prompt token IDs from HF as the control surface until tokenizer parity is proven.
- 2026-04-10: HF fast-tokenizer JSON can legitimately carry sparse token ID ranges, even when the tokenizer is otherwise healthy.
  Rule: do not enforce `maxTokenID + 1 == encoder.count` on `tokenizer.json` loads. Size the decoder by `maxTokenID + 1`, leave holes empty, and keep the strict contiguous-ID check only for legacy `vocab.json` / `merges.txt` GPT-2 fixtures.
- 2026-04-10: A grouped-dual-state proof artifact can look dramatically better under teacher-forced metrics while still failing the actual publication target.
  Rule: for recurrent-hybrid candidates, do not keep tuning once the stronger artifact still fails both axes on the real probe. In this lane, `stories-gds-12x128-v1-softdistill-a` reached `teacher_token_agreement=0.7955` and `KL=4.48`, yet the retained ANE probe still only measured `136.50 tok/s` and the benchmark prompt collapsed to token `0` for all `128` generated tokens.
- 2026-04-10: Do not leave `batch_size` as a dead config field in recurrent training loops.
  Rule: if a training spec exposes batching, consume batched windows in the optimizer step and add a wraparound batch-selection test immediately. Fake batching distorts the quality search and can hide a stability lever you need before declaring an architecture dead.
- 2026-04-10: The state-width knee still matters after the Gated DeltaNet pivot.
  Rule: do not assume the richer-state recurrent family wants the same width as grouped-dual-state. In the first `gdn-h1` ANE microbench sweep, `128` channels/head beat both `64` and `192`, so every new recurrent family needs its own width sweep before training starts.
- 2026-04-10: A recurrent-hybrid family is not "integrated" until a real exported artifact can run through the release probe with its metadata sidecar and checkpoint sidecar.
  Rule: before spending more training time on a new recurrent family, require an end-to-end dry-run export plus a release-binary `generate-recurrent` proof. For `gdn-h1`, that surfaced the real blocker cleanly: runtime dispatch and hybrid timing are now working, but the dry-run artifact still collapses and `.esp` recurrent-hybrid bundle packing remains unimplemented.
- 2026-04-10: Recurrent-hybrid smoke configs must respect the fixed runtime/store sequence contract.
  Rule: do not lower `student.max_seq` below the current `ModelConfig.seqLen` just to make a smoke run smaller. The `GDN1` checkpoint store still validates against the runtime sequence contract, and a mismatched smoke artifact will fail before benchmarking.
- 2026-04-10: A small KL improvement on a recurrent-hybrid smoke run does not mean the release probe path is recovering quality.
  Rule: for `gdn-h1`, require both free-running rollout improvement and benchmark-prompt probe improvement before keeping a training tweak. The `24`-step smoke run moved mean KL from `86.32` to `78.65`, but teacher agreement stayed at `1.86%` and the release probe still emitted token `0` for every generated step at only `77.41 tok/s`.
- 2026-04-10: Do not trust a recurrent-hybrid prompt-text probe until Swift prompt token IDs are checked against the authoritative HF tokenizer.
  Rule: for exported Stories artifacts, compare the release probe prompt IDs against Python/HF tokenization on at least one benchmark prompt before interpreting quality results. The `gdn-h1` probe looked completely dead until the tokenizer path was fixed; after the fix, `"Once upon a time"` correctly mapped to `[9038, 2501, 263, 931]`.
- 2026-04-10: Matching prompt token IDs is necessary but not sufficient for recurrent-hybrid parity.
  Rule: once prompt tokens match, immediately compare Python and Swift/ANE generation on the same exported checkpoint and same prompt IDs. On `gdn-h1`, Python generated repeated token `931` from the smoke checkpoint while the ANE probe still collapsed to token `0`, which means further training would have been wasted until runtime equivalence is proven.
- 2026-04-10: For multi-surface recurrent ANE kernels, never bind retained IOSurfaces by semantic guess order.
  Rule: ANE surface indices must follow alphabetical MIL tensor names, and prototype sessions should bind by explicit name rather than assuming the Swift semantic order matches runtime order. On `gdn-h1`, switching the dual-state prototype sessions to named alphabetical binding removed the all-`0` collapse and changed the benchmark-prompt output materially, which proves the old positional assumption was poisoning runtime evidence.
- 2026-04-10: A runtime-equivalence fix that turns one wrong decode into a different wrong decode is still progress, but only if you compare against the uncontaminated baseline immediately.
  Rule: after every parity patch, re-run the exact same retained artifact and compare Swift/ANE output against the Python-side baseline before claiming recovery. The `gdn-h1` I/O binding fix removed the all-`0` collapse, and the checkpoint RoPE fix changed the token stream again, but neither yet matched Python's repeated `931`; without that three-way comparison, it would be too easy to mistake movement for parity.
- 2026-04-10: For recurrent-hybrid artifacts with checkpoint schedules, a recurrent-only Python runner is not a valid parity oracle.
  Rule: when `checkpointSchedule` includes `swa:*`, reconstruct the Python reference from both the recurrent checkpoint and the exported checkpoint sidecar before comparing against Swift/ANE. On `gdn-h1`, the apparent parity gap disappeared once the Python runner was rebuilt from `weights/checkpoint_swa.bin`; the mixed runner matched Swift/ANE exactly, and the real blocker turned out to be quality, not runtime semantics.
- 2026-04-10: If a retained smoke checkpoint layer already matches Swift Metal attention against Swift CPU decode attention on hardware, stop blaming the low-level checkpoint kernel for generation collapse.
  Rule: once the layer-level hybrid decode test passes on the retained artifact, move the investigation up to artifact semantics or model quality. For `gdn-h1`, the smoke checkpoint layer matched on hardware, so more low-level checkpoint-kernel patching would have been wasted effort.
- 2026-04-10: Better teacher-forced metrics are not enough to keep a recurrent-hybrid family alive once rollout quality stays structurally bad across a bounded ablation ladder.
  Rule: if multiple focused variants improve teacher agreement and KL but still produce `0` passing rollout samples under an explicit `128`-token quality gate, demote the family and move priority to the next certified successor. On `gdn-h1`, the `quality-a/b/c` ladder improved teacher metrics substantially, but every run still failed the rollout gate.
- 2026-04-10: Once one family has a certified mixed Python runtime runner, build the same harness for the successor immediately.
  Rule: the replacement program should compare model families on the same standard. After `gdn-h1` parity closed, grouped-dual-state became the next active target; adding `load_grouped_dual_state_runtime_runner(...)` immediately exposed a real Swift/Python divergence on the retained fast-smoke artifact.
- 2026-04-10: For recurrent-hybrid parity work, argmax agreement through an early recurrent prefix is not enough evidence.
  Rule: once prompt-token IDs and generated tokens diverge, add prompt-prefix argmax traces and retained single-token layer traces immediately. On grouped-dual-state, the first hard split only became obvious after exposing `prompt_argmax_trace` in the probe and a retained Python single-token layer trace for token `9038`.
- 2026-04-10: When a full mixed recurrent-hybrid model disagrees with Python, prove the wrapper first before rewriting kernels.
  Rule: compare the full model against a manual session-by-session Swift chain on the same retained artifact and prompt. On grouped-dual-state, that test passed, which ruled out the high-level wrapper and moved the investigation down to tensor-level semantics.
- 2026-04-10: A checkpoint block can look innocent under Swift-only tests and still be the first real split against Python.
  Rule: Swift Metal-vs-CPU decode-attention parity is necessary but not sufficient. If the retained first checkpoint block is the first layer where Python and Swift hidden states diverge, do not treat Swift-only checkpoint tests as proof of Python equivalence.
- 2026-04-10: Layerwise argmax agreement can hide a dangerous hidden-state drift in recurrent-hybrid parity work.
  Rule: once Swift and Python agree on layerwise argmax through a recurrent prefix, compare hidden tensors before declaring that prefix closed. On grouped-dual-state token `9038`, layer `2` still carried about `0.0114` max abs hidden drift versus Python even though both sides kept argmax `9038`, and that was enough to send the first checkpoint layer into a completely different token family.
- 2026-04-10: A per-head square weight tensor (equal in/out dims) whose Python einsum contracts the *middle* axis is indistinguishable from the Swift grouped-conv `(out, in)` convention by shape alone. The `grouped-dual-state` cell declared `WcarryX`, `WcarryD`, and `Wo` as `(head_count, channels_per_head, channels_per_head) = (12, 64, 64)` and consumed them via einsum `"bhc,hcd->bhd"` where `c` is the contracted (input) axis in the middle and `d` is the output axis last. Swift's conv kernel expected the middle axis to be output and the last to be input — the opposite convention. Because the two inner dimensions are equal, every static shape check passed and the bug only surfaced as a ~0.011 hidden-state drift at layer 2 amplifying to ~0.7 at the first softmax checkpoint. The fix was a per-head in-place transpose at the Swift checkpoint loader; four other grouped-dual-state weights with asymmetric shapes (`WmemQ`, `WmemS`, `WmemV`, `WcarryM`) did not need the fix because their einsum convention already matched Swift's conv convention.
  Rule: when a new recurrent cell family uses per-head square weight tensors with equal in/out dimensions, add a loader-level assertion that pins the expected contraction axis against the Python einsum pattern, and write a pre-fix parity gate that compares the Swift CPU reference against a Python oracle at ≤1e-3 tolerance before declaring the runtime certified. Swift-vs-Swift self-consistency tests are blind to this class of bug because both paths share the same wrong convention.
- 2026-04-10: When running large `ANE_HARDWARE_TESTS=1` suites in a single process, the per-process ANE compile budget (~100) is exhausted and late tests fail with `"ANE compile budget exhausted — exec() restart required"`. This is a pre-existing harness limitation, not a regression. Running the affected tests in isolation makes them pass. Rule: for full-suite hardware verification, either split the suite across multiple `swift test --filter` invocations or accept that the last N tests may need re-runs in isolation; do not interpret compile-budget failures as correctness regressions.
- 2026-04-10: FP16 ANE parity tolerance vs FP32 CPU parity tolerance must be budgeted separately. On the grouped-dual-state smoke checkpoint after closing the weight-layout bug, the Swift CPU reference matched Python at ≤1e-3 per layer (FP32 vs FP32) while the ANE path drifted up to ~3e-3 by layer 10 due to per-layer fp16 ulp of ~1e-4 compounding through 11 recurrent layers. Rule: new ANE parity gates against a Python oracle should budget 5x the CPU tolerance (typically 1e-3 → 5e-3) with an explicit code comment explaining the FP16 precision envelope; a regression here means ulp budget was exceeded, not that the cell math is wrong.
- 2026-04-10: The SWA checkpoint cost dominates the per-token budget on a 12L+3SWA grouped-dual-state config at ~52% (1.77 ms/SWA-layer vs 0.37 ms/GDS-layer fused-triplet on M3 Max). Removing the SWA layers entirely and shipping a pure-recurrent 3L+0SWA or 4L+0SWA config is the architectural path to the 300+ tok/s replacement-program target on this hardware. The 12L+3SWA lane is architecturally capped at ~100 tok/s even with every runtime optimization applied.
  Rule: for publication-target grouped-dual-state configs, default to no-SWA checkpoint schedules with 3-4 GDS layers. The 12-layer + 3-SWA recipe is only useful as a quality reference for validating smaller models, not as a publication candidate.
- 2026-04-10: Layer extraction from a trained 12L+3SWA grouped-dual-state checkpoint into a smaller config does NOT work as a shortcut for retraining. Extracted 3L/4L/6L variants of softdistill-a produce identity collapse (echo prompt tokens, then lock onto the last prompt token forever) because the output head (final RMSNorm + classifier) was trained to consume layer-11's deeply-processed representation, not layer-2/3/6's mid-network features. The 3L extracted variant hits 420-457 tok/s but generates `[931, 931, 931, ...]` regardless of input.
  Rule: to ship a smaller grouped-dual-state config, retrain from scratch with the target architecture using the same distill recipe. Training a 3L+0SWA from scratch takes ~5 minutes on M3 Max MPS and produces demonstrably coherent output, whereas layer extraction takes 1 second but produces unusable models.
- 2026-04-10: The Espresso teacher bundle path `.build/release-bundles/stories110m-smoke.esp/weights` referenced by the distill scripts is NOT the canonical teacher location on a clean host. The actual retained teacher weights live at `~/Library/Application Support/Espresso/demo/stories110m/` with the required metadata.json / embeddings/ / layers/ / lm_head.bin / final_norm.bin / tokenizer files.   Rule: when a distill config fails with `FileNotFoundError: .build/release-bundles/stories110m-smoke.esp/weights/metadata.json`, check for the demo teacher at `~/Library/Application Support/Espresso/demo/stories110m/` before assuming the teacher is missing. Update the config's weights_dir and tokenizer_dir to point at that path.
- 2026-04-10: Parity work can unlock latent quality in checkpoints that were previously judged as "failed". The retained softdistill-a 12L+3SWA checkpoint was rejected in the prior session as "producing token 0 for all 128 generated tokens" and was slated for kill. The 2026-04-10 transpose fix (Milestone C) reverted that judgment entirely — post-fix, the same checkpoint produces `"Once upon a time, a time, a, there a lived, there was a"` which is recognizable undertrained English.   Rule: when parity is closed on a runtime lane that was previously judged dead for quality, re-probe every retained checkpoint in that lane before committing to retrain. The transpose fix unlocked softdistill-a's 0.7955 teacher_token_agreement that was invisible before the fix.
- 2026-04-10: The ANE RMSNorm+classifier fused head is a modest win on 12L grouped-dual-state (~7% tok/s uplift) because the head bucket is only 17% of the per-token budget. On shallower configs where the head bucket is a larger fraction of the budget, the ANE head becomes more valuable. Measured on this host: 3L no-SWA with CPU head = 472 tok/s; 3L no-SWA with ANE head = 562 tok/s (19% uplift). The fused head's value scales inversely with trunk depth.
  Rule: when evaluating output-head-backend choices, compute the head bucket as a fraction of the total per-token budget. If head < 20% of budget, ANE head wins are marginal. If head > 40% of budget (shallow trunk or large vocab), ANE head is a dominant throughput lever.

## 2026-04-11 — Corpus surgery breaks learned cross-prompt attractors

A learned cross-prompt terminal attractor (v6's `"Ready, little one?" she asked, her voice a little shaky.` appearing in 18+ of 24 prompts) is NOT a single memorized phrase — it is a combination of multiple low-frequency corpus patterns that the model learns to co-activate under greedy argmax. Component counts in v6's 2500-story medgemma corpus: "voice a little shaky" 13, `"?", she asked, her voice` 38, "little one" 150, exact "Ready...little one" 5. Individually rare, together they form a basin the decoder falls into.

**Rule**: When diagnosing a cross-prompt terminal attractor, grep for each *component* of the attractor phrase as an independent regex. Drop any training story containing any component — do NOT attempt to preserve story completeness. For v7, dropping 238/2500 (9.5%) of the medgemma corpus on 8 component regexes upgraded the strict analyzer verdict from FAIL to PARTIAL_PUBLICATION, with `publication_coherent` 1→5 and median coherent prefix 75.5→128 tokens, at a +9.8% throughput improvement as a side benefit.

**Why**: Greedy argmax amplifies whichever modal ending has the highest accumulated probability mass. If the corpus has enough component variants of a sentimental close (dialogue tag + voice descriptor + address), their modes compose into a single deterministic attractor even though no exact phrase was memorized. Removing the components starves the signal instead of hoping regularization will fix it.

**How to apply**: For any recurrent/hybrid decode lane that produces a recognizable terminal catchphrase at rollout time: (1) grep the training corpus for each sub-phrase separately; (2) treat anything occurring in 1%+ of stories as a suspect component; (3) filter-drop stories containing any component (do not try to locally edit stories — it is not worth the complexity); (4) retrain and re-run the strict analyzer before trying architectural or regularization fixes. Corpus surgery is a causal intervention; training-loop fixes are indirect.

## 2026-04-11 — Over-extending corpus surgery can swap a terminal attractor for mid-story filler collapse

v8 extended the v7 medgemma filter with `Look, Grandpa`, `Of course, sweetie`, `bright eyes`, `eyes wide with wonder`, `wise old owl`, and `shiny things`. The new corpus kept 2164/2262 stories, parity stayed closed, and throughput held at `583.53 tok/s`, but strict quality regressed from v7's `5/24` publication-coherent to `4/24`, with degenerates rising from `4/24` to `6/24`. The failure mode changed: instead of the old terminal phrase family, the model fell into mid-story filler loops like `"kind woman with a kind woman"`, `"ate and ate"`, and `"He imagined the storm's spine"` across multiple prompts.

**Rule**: once corpus surgery starts removing broad narrative scaffold phrases rather than narrow attractor components, stop extending the same filter family. That kind of pruning can starve the model of connective tissue and push greedy argmax into generic filler loops even while parity and throughput remain strong. After one such regression, the next candidate should restore scaffold diversity with a mixed corpus or move to more capacity, not keep widening the regex list.

v9 interleaved the retained filtered medgemma corpus with a size-matched TinyStories slice (2262 + 2262 stories).
